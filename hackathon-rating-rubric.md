# Agora Voice AI Hackathon Manila 2026 - Judging Rubric

## Overview

This rubric provides a structured framework for evaluating hackathon submissions. Each project will be scored across multiple categories on a scale of 1-5, with specific criteria for each score level.

***

## Project Requirements

### Required Technologies

All submissions **must** use the following technologies:

- **Agora RTC SDK**: Low-latency real-time audio and video communication
- **Agora Conversational AI Engine**: Hosted platform for building real-time voice AI agents
- **TRAE IDE**: AI-assisted development environment for planning, coding, debugging, and documentation

### Project Rules

- All projects must integrate Agora technologies
- All source code must be created during the hackathon period
- Teams are must use TRAE IDE for development support and workflow management
- Third-party libraries and APIs are allowed with proper attribution
- Projects must follow data privacy and ethical AI guidelines
- Teams must submit working demos and documentation

***

## Evaluation Process

The evaluation process consists of two rounds: an initial technical screening of all submissions, followed by final stage presentations for the top teams.

### Evaluation Phases

**Round 1: Technical Screening**\
Judges review code, demos, and documentation for all submissions to select the top finalists.

**Round 2: Final Presentations**\
The top (5) five selected teams from each group will present their solutions to the full panel.

**Final Deliberation**\
Judges review scores, discuss finalists, and select the winners.

**Awards Announcement**\
Winners announced with technical and product feedback

### Scoring Methodology

Round 1 uses a 1-5 scale:

- 5 (Exceptional): Exceeds expectations, innovative approach, production-ready
- 4 (Very Good): Strong implementation with minor areas for improvement
- 3 (Good): Meets requirements with solid execution
- 2 (Basic): Functional but lacks polish or depth
- 1 (Needs Work): Incomplete or significant issues

Round 2 uses direct percentage scoring (max 100 points):

- Each criterion is scored out of its allocated weight (e.g., 25/25 pts).
- Final winners are determined by the total points in Round 2.

***

## Scoring Categories

### Round 1: Initial Phase (Technical Screening)

#### 30%. Integration & Use of Agora Technologies

_Agora's Conversational AI Engine and/or RTC is meaningfully and centrally integrated into the product, as verified from the submitted codebase and demo video._

#### 25%. System Functionality

_The product works end-to-end reliably, as demonstrated through the submitted demo video and repository without team assistance._

#### 20%. Technical Architecture

_System design, technology choices, and scalability considerations are sound and assessable from the codebase and documentation alone._

#### 15%. Originality of Concept

_Evaluates the freshness and distinctiveness of the idea — judges assess whether the solution offers a novel approach to Voice AI that goes beyond common or predictable use cases._

#### 10%. Submission Compliance

_Evaluates adherence to the required GitHub repository format and boilerplate structure as specified in the submission guidelines._

### Round 2: Final Phase (Presentations)

#### 25%. Functionality & Demo Quality

_Code quality, completeness, polish, and reliability of the submission, including a fully functional and stable live demo with complete features._

#### 25%. Innovation and Creativity

_Creativity, uniqueness, novelty of idea combined with real-world value and relevance._

#### 20%. Impact & Real-World Application

_Potential for solving real problems through process automation or user engagement._

#### 10%. Sustainability & Feasibility

_Community value, enterprise applicability, real-world relevance sustainability and business viability._

#### 20%. Presentation & Pitch

_Clear, engaging delivery of problem, solution, and demo with strong storytelling and visuals._

## Final Score Calculation

### Round 1: Initial Phase

- Each category score (1-5) is multiplied by its percentage weight (decimal)
- The weighted scores are summed to calculate the final score (maximum 5.0)

### Round 2: Final Phase

- Each category is scored directly based on its allocated points (maximum score = weight percentage)
- The scores for all categories are summed to calculate the total final score (maximum 100 points)
